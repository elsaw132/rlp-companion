Generated from real output: each persona below was run through the live /api/stage2-reveal route, exactly as the screen does. The findings are placed verbatim from the locked library; only the lead-in is written by Vita.

How to read this

Read each card against the three checks from rule F, and tick as you go. A card that fails 1 or 2 means the trigger predicate is too loose — tighten it. A card that fails 3 means the stat shouldn't be in the reveal pool at all.

1. Anchor is real & specific — does the lead-in name something actually in this person's data, in recognisable language? Not a category, not invented.
2. Subject matches — is the finding's subject the same thing the anchor is about? (A strength finding anchored to "Walking the dog" fails this.)
3. Lands gain / lever / curiosity — no mortality or disease number reached the card or the tap.

Personas are constructed, not real pilots — deliberately shaped to hit the edges (nothing picked, one pick, everything picked). That's what makes a loose predicate show itself; real pilot data confirms the distribution later.
