Components

Buttons

Navy is the standard primary action. Orange is used only on the current step's "Continue". A button is at least 48px tall.

Accessibility: the bright accent #E06F1F with white text only passes AA for large text. Button fills use --accent-strong #B85C16 (shown above) so the white label meets 4.5:1. Keep the bright --accent for borders, the active dot, and surfaces — not behind small white text.

Components · extends the system

Conversation bubbles

Not shown in the dashboard mock — derived from the same palette for the session screen. Vita speaks in warm cream; the user replies in bordered white. One question at a time.

A single day gives us a snapshot. A week gives us a rhythm. When you picture a really good week in retirement — what's the one thing you'd want every week to have in it?
Probably a long walk somewhere green. And no alarm clock.
The walk and the no-alarm both point at the same thing — mornings that belong to you. Hold onto that. Is that every day, or are there days you'd happily get up early for?
Using the system

Six rules to hold the look together

1. Orange is the cursor. Exactly one orange element on screen — the current step. Everything done is navy; everything future is grey.
2. Serif feels, sans functions. If a user is meant to feel it, it's serif. If it's a control or a label, it's sans.
3. Cream is the coach. Warm surfaces appear only where Vita is present or speaking. The rest of the app is white.
4. Lift is meaningful. Most cards sit flat with a hairline. Shadow signals "this is where you are."
5. The band is theirs. The yellow header and logo are the provider's. Never put product chrome in the band.
6. Calm, not clinical. Generous spacing, gentle illustration, no dense data, no progress shame.
